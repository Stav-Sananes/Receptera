<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| process → env | pydantic-settings reads RECEPTRA_* env vars from the process environment (and optionally .env). Any untrusted env var becomes configuration. |
| container → localhost | `/healthz` binds 0.0.0.0:8080 inside the container; exposed only via explicit compose port mapping. |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-01-02-01 | Information Disclosure | Settings logged on startup could leak secrets | mitigate | Only `model_dir`, `ollama_host`, `chroma_host` (all non-secret infrastructure paths) are logged; no credentials live in the Settings object in Phase 1. When secrets are added in later phases, they MUST be explicitly excluded from the startup log statement. |
| T-01-02-02 | Tampering | `extra="ignore"` on SettingsConfigDict means extra env vars are silently dropped | accept | This prevents a typo like `RECEPTRA_MODELDIR` (missing underscore) from raising a startup error and blocking the whole service in production. Tradeoff: a misconfigured var is silently ignored. Mitigated by explicit logging of loaded config values in `_log_config`. |
| T-01-02-03 | Denial of Service | Uvicorn binds 0.0.0.0 in `__main__.py` | accept | Binding all interfaces is intentional for Docker; the attack surface is constrained by `docker-compose.yml` port mapping (Plan 04) and the service is local-only per the project's privacy constraint. Documented with a noqa + comment. |
| T-01-02-04 | Supply Chain | A dependency listed in pyproject.toml could introduce a GPL license transitively | mitigate | Plan 06 adds `pip-licenses --allow-only` to CI. This plan only pins versions; enforcement lives in the CI plan. |
</threat_model>
